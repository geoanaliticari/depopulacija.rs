11. Accessibility as a Determinant of Depopulation

Accessibility is a factor that significantly determines the attractiveness of an area for settling and overall development, and it indirectly affects its population dynamics. Traffic-isolated areas are exposed to continuous negative changes, while accessible areas have a more favourable population trend.
