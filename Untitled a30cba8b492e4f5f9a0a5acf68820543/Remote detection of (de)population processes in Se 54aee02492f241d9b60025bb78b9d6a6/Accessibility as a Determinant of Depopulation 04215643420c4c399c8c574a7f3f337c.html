Accessibility as a Determinant of Depopulation

The indicator Accessibility as a Determinant of Depopulation makes possible to estimate the accessibility of a certain area that directly contributes to its attractiveness, influencing population concentration and overall development trends. At the same time, it takes into account the road network density, the quality of roads, the terrain morphometry, and the settlement’s zone of influence. To calculate the indicator, data on the average travel time to the municipal centre (OpenStreetMap), based on the road network with classified categories and average speeds of motor vehicles on different categories of roads was used. The average time distance from settlement to the municipal center at the level of Serbia is 21.3 min, which served as a threshold value when classifying settlements according to this parameter. This data was overlapped with the Population Change Index 2015/1975 (GHS- POP R2019A - GHS population grid multitemporal [European Commission, Joint Research Center, 2019]) and administrative division (GeoSrbija [Open data of the National Data Infrastructure, Republic Geodetic Authority, n.d.]) in order to illustrate the interdependence of population changes and road accessibility and a deeper understanding of the depopulation process in that period. The territory of Serbia is classified into four categories with different ratio of the observed parameters, which provides the possibility of identifying areas that were demographically endangered due to road isolation.

The zones characterised by a dense road network, favourable terrain morphology, and proximity to regional centres are marked by a significant population increase. They are located in the Novi Sad and Belgrade region, the Peri-Pannonian realm, Šumadija and Pomoravlje (Kragujevac, Jagodina, Čačak, Kraljevo, Kruševac, Niš) and the vital zone of southwestern Serbia (Novi Pazar, Tutin). These characteristics are shared by 12.2% of the total number of settlements. On the other hand, the zones identified as inaccessible but marked by a population increase (6.9% of settlements) are mainly located in the southern part of the country, in the municipalities of Kraljevo, Čačak, Tutin, Novi Pazar, Kruševac, Niš, Jagodina, etc. For this group, the travel time to the municipal centre is longer due to the terrain features, but the influence of the urban centre is reflected in a positive population trend. The accessible zones that are weaker in terms of population include the so-called “second peripheral zones”, which are far from the main traffic routes, but still have a good accessibility at the local level. The most unfavourable features are observed in the border areas of the country, as well as in the peripheral parts of municipalities. This indicates that population shrinkage becomes more intensive as one moves away from the centre. This occurs in one-third of all settlements in Serbia (32.1%), usually in hilly and mountainous areas.
