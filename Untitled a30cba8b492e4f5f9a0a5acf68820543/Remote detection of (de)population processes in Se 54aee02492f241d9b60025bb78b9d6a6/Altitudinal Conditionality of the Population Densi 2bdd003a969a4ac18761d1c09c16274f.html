Altitudinal Conditionality of the Population Density’ Changes

The indicator Altitudinal Conditionality of the Population Density' Changes is used to calculate and analyses population concentration according to altitude zones. It implies an overlap of the hypsometric structure of spatial units and population density, based on which the altitude limits for sparsely populated and demographic shrinkage zones are defined.A set of terrain morphometry data (EU-DEM v1.1 [European Environment Agency, 2016]), grid population data (GHS-POP R2019A - GHS population grid multitemporal [European Commission, Joint Research Centre]) by administrative units (GeoSrbija [Open data of the National Data Infrastructure, Republic Geodetic Authority]) were used.

The highest population concentration in Serbia is observed in the 200–500 m a.s.l. zone, as well as in the lowland zone (up to 200 m). Most urban settlements and municipal centres are located in these two zones (Belgrade, Valjevo, Kragujevac, Ljig, Niš, Novi Sad, etc.). As much as 21.3% of the settlements belong to the plains, while 26.5% are distinctly mountainous. With increasing altitude, the number of high-density settlements decreases. In the third hypsometric zone, 500–800 m a.s.l., a high population density is observed in southwestern and southeastern Serbia, and these settlements are partly urban. As far as municipal centres are concerned, only Nova Varoš, Tutin, Čajetina and Crna Trava have a high population concentration in the 800–1000 m a.s.l. zone, whereas in Sjenica population concentration is high in the 1000–1500 m a.s.l. zone. The number of settlements at higher altitudes (above 1500 m a.s.l.) is significantly reduced (115 settlements). In these settlements, population concentration is lower, with the exception of some settlements in predominantly mountainous municipalities – Bosilegrad, Crna Trava, Gadžin Han, Tutin, Sjenica, Novi Pazar, Raška, Brus, Knjaževac.
