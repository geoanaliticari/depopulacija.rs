Hypsometric Structure (Altitudinal Zonality) of Municipalities and Settlements

Depopulation is mainly associated with hilly and mountainous areas, which prevail in southern parts of Serbia. As the altitude increases, the number of settlements steadily declines, while lower zones are significantly more represented among settled areas. A hypsometric structure of Serbia’s territory according to settlements/municipalities and individual altitude zones (0–200 m, 200–500 m, 500–800 m, 800–1000 m, 1000–1500 m, > 1500 m a.s.l.) was defined and the average altitude was determined. To calculate this indicator, a digital terrain model EU-DEM v1.1 [European Environment Agency] was combined with an administrative division (GeoSrbija [National Data Infrastructure, Republic Geodetic Authority]).

Serbia is a predominantly hilly country with an average altitude of 459 m a.s.l. and the mountain hypsometric zone (200–500 m a.s.l.) as the most dominant belt. There are differences within spatial units and they are reflected in the variability of hypsometric structures at the settlement and municipal levels. A significant number of settlements are concentrated in the first three altitude zones. Above 800 m a.s.l., they are three times fewer in number. Only three settlements can be found above 1500 m a.s.l. and these are mountain tourist centres (Kopaonik Mt. and Stara Planina Mt.) which sustainability is ensured by winter tourism activities. It may be observed that the altitude of 800 m a.s.l. is the upper threshold for settlement distribution. Above that altitude limit settlements are barely sustainable and demographically endangered.
